How a Partnership Works

1
Initial Consultation
Our team meets with the landowner to understand the land's potential, current condition, and the landowner's goals for the partnership.
2
Land & Soil Assessment
Detailed soil testing, water availability study, and crop suitability analysis by our agronomists to determine the optimal farming model.
3
Crop & Revenue Projection
We present a detailed crop plan, investment breakdown, expected yield, and projected profit-sharing figures for the landowner's review.
4
Partnership Agreement Signed
A registered partnership deed is executed covering crop plan, profit-sharing ratios, reporting schedule, and exit provisions.
5
Cultivation Begins
Agri-Land invests in soil preparation, seeds, water systems, and deploys local farmers for cultivation — landowner has full visibility throughout.
6
Harvest, Sale & Profit Sharing
Post-harvest, produce is sold via premium channels. Revenue accounts are shared transparently and landowner's share is transferred within 15 days.
Is Your Land Eligible for Partnership?
Agricultural land of 2+ acres (contiguous preferred)
Clear and undisputed land title in your name
Located in Tamil Nadu (all districts considered)
Basic water access (borewell, canal, or rainwater)
No active court disputes or encumbrances on the land
Landowner willing to engage for minimum 3–year term
Fallow, unused, or underperforming land is welcome
